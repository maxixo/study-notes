The Concept: A decision tree starts by dividing data (like houses) into categories
. For example, it might group houses by the number of bedrooms to predict their price
.
Training the Model: The process of using historical data to find these patterns is called fitting or training the model
.
Improving Accuracy: A simple tree might only look at one factor, but "deeper" trees add more splits to consider things like lot size or location
.
The Prediction: To find a price, you follow the path that matches a house's features until you reach the bottom, which is called a leaf
.
